Alaska Energy Infrastructure

The Alaska Energy Regeneration (AER) project is a statewide initiative aimed at advancing sustainable living in response to the escalating climate crisis. Spanning 375 million acres and home to 740,000 residents across approximatly 200 communities, Alaska faces unique challenges in transitioning to renewable energy. AER will develop tailored, community-driven sustainability solutions through data collection, applied research, and policy development. Sustainability Assessments (SAs)—conducted via focus groups, surveys, and town halls with the guidance of Indigenous communities—will provide insights into existing practices and environmental needs. Findings will be shared in real time through the Alaska Energy Regeneration Network (AERN), a collaborative platform connecting residents, researchers, and decision-makers. A strong digital presence will support ongoing public engagement. The project will culminate in the Alaska Energy Regeneration 2025 Report, offering actionable, evidence-based policy alternatives ahead of the 2026 legislative session. AER invites collaboration from a wide range of stakeholders committed to a resilient, sustainable future for Alaska.
