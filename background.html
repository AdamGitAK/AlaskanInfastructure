Alaskan Energy Infrastructure (AEI) : Advancing Community Driven Renewable Energy

Project Summary

The Alaska Energy Regeneration (AER) project is a statewide, including remote and energy challenged, initiative designed to accelerate the transition to lasting/energy infrastructure/renewable. Alaska remote and diverse communities. In a state spanning 375 million acres and home to about 740,000 residents across 200+ diverse communities, Alaskan energy stability poses unique challenges. Many communities are disconnected from centralized infrastructure/grid and climate adaptation. Stable energy implementation must be adaptable, collaborative, and data-informed.

Just like we have had long term oil and gas stability, as our resources and landscape have changed, Alaska will adapt to new energy infrastructure that provides the same long-term security.
