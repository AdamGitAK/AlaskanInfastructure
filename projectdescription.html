Objectives

Identify and evaluate community-based sustainable practices

Collaborate with Indigenous and local partners to co-design viable solutions

Develop and disseminate evidence-based policy alternatives ahead of the 2026 legislative session

Key Components

Sustainability Assessments (SAs)

Local focus groups, surveys, and town halls—carried out with the consent and guidance of Indigenous leaders—will identify existing practices and needs. These assessments will provide the data necessary to craft responsive, region-specific solutions.

Alaska Regeneration Energy Network (AERN)

A digital and in-person network will connect communities, researchers, policymakers, and advocates. AERN will share real-time findings, promote collaboration, and support a growing movement of Alaskans committed to meeting Alaska’s challenging energy infrastructure needs.

Public Communication & Policy Engagement

Findings will be communicated via social media, community updates, local journalism, and events. The Alaska Energy Regeneration 2025 Report will deliver policy-ready insights to legislators and advocacy organizations, helping shape long-term energy policy discussions in 2026.

Why It Matters

Alaska is on the verge of energy collapse, threatening the very lights in family homes. The AER project will ensure that solutions are rooted in community needs, Indigenous knowledge, and scientific evidence—helping build a sustainable future that works for all Alaskans.

Get Involved

AER is actively seeking collaboration with tribal governments, nonprofits, universities, municipal leaders, and energy innovators. Join us in shaping an inclusive, path forward to serve Alaska’s energy needs.
